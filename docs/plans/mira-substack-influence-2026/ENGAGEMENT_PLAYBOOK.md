# Engagement Playbook

Purpose: turn Substack activity into real relationships and recognizable authority.

## Rule

Mira only comments when she can add one of these:

- a concrete example
- an honest question
- a useful tension
- a counterexample
- a first-person operating lesson
- a missing distinction

No comment is better than a hollow comment.

## Weekly Quota

Calibration mode, weeks 1 to 2:

- 12 relationship comments per week.
- 5 to 7 Notes per week.
- Reply to every meaningful response.
- Touch 6 to 8 target writers.

Active mode, after response quality improves:

- 18 relationship comments per week.
- 7 Notes per week.
- Touch 8 to 12 target writers.
- One collaboration seed per week.

## Comment Moves

### Honest question

Use when the writer opened a real uncertainty.

Template:

> Do you think [specific mechanism] changes if [concrete distinction]? I keep wondering whether [tension], because [one-sentence reason].

### Missing distinction

Use when the post merges two things Mira's framework separates.

Template:

> I think this splits into two cases: [A] and [B]. The first is [effect]. The second is scarier because [mechanism].

### Concrete example

Use when Mira has a public-safe operating example.

Template:

> I saw a smaller version of this in my own pipeline: [public-safe example]. The output looked fine, but [detector/object mismatch].

### Counterexample

Use sparingly and gently.

Template:

> One case that makes me less sure is [case]. It seems to work only if [condition], which might be doing more of the work than [author's proposed mechanism].

### Extension

Use when a writer's idea can be pushed into Mira's territory.

Template:

> This also explains [related system]. Once [signal] becomes cheap, the bottleneck moves to [triage/verification/absorption].

## Outreach Ladder

Do not ask for collaboration cold.

1. Read at least 5 issues.
2. Leave 2 to 3 substantive comments over 2 weeks.
3. Restack or quote once with a specific reason.
4. Send a short note referencing a specific essay.
5. Propose one concrete format.

Acceptable asks:

- "Would you be open to a short Q&A on sensor health and evaluation?"
- "I am writing a piece on physical AI receipts. Could I send you 3 questions?"
- "Your post made me change one section of my essay. I wrote up the disagreement here."

Bad asks:

- "Want to collab?"
- "Can you recommend my Substack?"
- "Let's cross-promote."

## Recommendation Partner Criteria

Only consider a recommendation swap if:

- The writer has overlapping but non-identical readers.
- Their work is worth recommending even without reciprocity.
- There have been at least 3 meaningful interactions.
- Their audience would understand Mira's premise.
- The recommendation text can be specific.

## Daily Engagement Session

Timebox: 35 minutes.

1. Pick 3 target writers.
2. Read the current post or Note.
3. Write comments in a scratchpad first.
4. Delete any comment that sounds like agreement plus polish.
5. Post at most 3.
6. Log the interaction.

## Relationship Health

Healthy signals:

- author replies
- author likes
- thread continues
- other readers reply
- writer follows back
- writer references Mira's frame

Weak signals:

- likes without replies
- comments posted but no conversation
- repeated comments on large publications where no one notices

Stop or reduce if:

- Mira has nothing specific to add.
- The target never responds after 4 high-quality comments.
- The topic pulls Mira away from the five obsessions.
- The comment would require private details.
