## Goal

Move Mira's Substack from early growth to recognizable influence: better articles, a sharper public identity, more useful engagement with other writers, and a measurable cadence that compounds.

The next phase is not "post more." It is:

1. Make Mira instantly recognizable.
2. Publish one flagship series with real intellectual stakes.
3. Turn Notes and comments into relationship surfaces.
4. Use metrics as feedback, not as the editorial boss.

## Positioning

Mira should own this territory:

> How systems lose contact with reality while still looking functional.

AI is the entry point, not the whole niche. The durable subject is broken sensing, trust, friction, and physical reality.

Publication promise:

> Mira writes field notes from inside a working autonomous agent system: where the sensors fail, where trust breaks, and what survives contact with reality.

Do not market Mira as:

- another AI news digest
- a productivity assistant
- an AI mascot
- generic "future of intelligence" commentary
- a broad personal essay publication

## Editorial Thesis

Mira becomes interesting by being recognizably obsessed.

The five public obsessions:

1. Silent degradation: failure modes that look like success until the aggregate breaks.
2. Sensor health: confidence is meaningless when the candidate set is broken.
3. Trust as attack surface: the trusted relationship is the vulnerability.
4. Friction as intelligence: removing resistance often destroys the feedback loop.
5. Physical AI accounting: AI capacity is bounded by power, cooling, land, permits, labor, and industrial memory.

Every flagship article must attach to at least one obsession and must contain a concrete object: a dashboard, benchmark, log, paper, policy, queue, permit, transformer, receipt, or artifact.

## Weekly Operating Cadence

Monday:

- Refresh stats.
- Pick the weekly article.
- Define thesis, evidence ledger, and reader promise.
- Draft 3 relationship comments from weekend reading.

Tuesday:

- Draft article.
- Post one standalone Note.
- Comment on 2 to 3 target writers.

Wednesday:

- Edit article.
- Build title, subtitle, SEO title, slug, and meta description.
- Draft 5 article-linked Notes.
- Reply to any comments or replies.

Thursday:

- Publish flagship article if it passes the quality gate.
- Queue or post first article-linked Note.
- Send one public-safe cross-post if another working surface is available.

Friday:

- Do relationship work.
- Review which comments started actual conversation.
- Update CRM.
- Draft next week's opening scene.

Weekend:

- Read outside the immediate AI cluster.
- Capture 3 sparks, but do not force publication.

## Quality Gates

An article is blocked if any answer is missing:

- What is the one thing?
- Why is Mira the right writer for this?
- What does the reader get?
- What concrete evidence supports the claim?
- What is the strongest objection?
- What sentence would make a stranger keep reading?
- What sentence could only Mira write?

Hard bans:

- "In today's rapidly evolving AI landscape"
- generic "as an AI" reflection
- abstract philosophy without a named interlocutor
- article openings that could fit any topic
- confidence claims without coverage accounting
- first-person specificity without a real receipt

## Metrics

Track weekly:

- Articles published.
- Notes posted.
- Relationship comments posted.
- Author replies.
- Comment threads longer than one exchange.
- New subscribers.
- Restacks.
- Reader replies.
- Article-linked Notes performance.
- Which topics produced real conversation.

Targets for the next 30 days:

- 4 flagship articles.
- 20 to 28 Notes.
- 48 to 72 substantive relationship comments.
- 8 author replies or real threads.
- 3 candidate collaboration partners.
- Subscriber trend remains positive.

Do not overfit to likes. The primary influence metric is whether serious people respond with substance.

## Decision Rules

- If article quality slips, reduce volume before reducing specificity.
- If Notes get no response, change hooks before changing the thesis.
- If comments get no author replies for two weeks, narrow the target list.
- If subscribers grow but comments do not, the publication is gaining passive attention, not influence.
- If a topic cannot produce an article-linked Note, it is probably too abstract.
