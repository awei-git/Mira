# Hook: First Line

**Tags:** writing, substack, newsletter, hooks, engagement

## Core Principle
Open every newsletter with a single sentence that forces the reader to continue — curiosity, tension, or a violated assumption. The first sentence is the only sentence that matters initially.

## Five Proven Opener Patterns
1. **Bold counter-intuitive claim** — "Everything you know about X is wrong."
2. **Unresolved tension** — "It started with a mistake I didn't catch for three months."
3. **In-medias-res scene drop** — Drop into a vivid, specific moment. No setup.
4. **Pain-naming question** — A question that names an exact, specific pain the reader has.
5. **Striking statistic reframed as consequence** — Not the number, the implication of the number.

## Rules
- Never open with context-setting or throat-clearing ("In today's issue…", "Welcome back!"). Obligation statements kill momentum.
- The hook must make a promise the piece will keep — bait-and-switch destroys trust and causes early drop-off even when the body is strong.
- Write 5 candidate first lines for every piece before choosing. The first draft is almost never the best.
- Test with the "so what?" filter: if someone can read the first line and have no reason to read the second, rewrite it.

## Structure After the Hook
- **Hook** (1 sentence): creates pull
- **Lede** (2–3 sentences): establishes context and stakes
- **Transition**: delivers on the hook's promise
