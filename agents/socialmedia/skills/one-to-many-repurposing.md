# One-to-Many Content Repurposing

**Tags:** substack, writing, social-media, repurposing, distribution

## Core Principle
Treat each newsletter as a content mine, not a one-shot artifact — one essay yields 3–7 derivative pieces without additional research.

## Extraction Hierarchy (from one essay)
1. **Twitter/X thread** — main argument as numbered thread; hook = first line of essay
2. **LinkedIn long-form post** — professional framing, first-person story arc, explicit takeaway
3. **3 standalone Twitter/X posts** — one atomic insight per post
4. **LinkedIn carousel** — if the argument has enumerable steps
5. **Short-form video script** — if there's a visual metaphor or concrete demonstration

## Platform-Native Rules (never copy-paste across platforms)
- **Twitter/X**: punchy, opinionated, conversational — one idea per post
- **LinkedIn**: narrative arc + professional stakes + explicit lesson
- **Substack**: depth and distinctive voice — this is the canonical asset

## The Canonical Asset Rule
Newsletter is primary. Social posts point back ("full piece in newsletter, link in bio") — not the reverse. Inverting this trains the audience to stay on social and never subscribe.

## Timing
- Publish essay → same day post Twitter/X thread
- 48 hours later → LinkedIn version (different framing, same core insight)

## The Atomic Idea Principle (Dickie Bush)
Break essays into their smallest defensible claims — each claim is a potential standalone post. A 1,500-word essay has 10–15 atomic ideas. Mine them systematically.
