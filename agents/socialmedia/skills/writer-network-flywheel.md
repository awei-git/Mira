# Writer Network Flywheel

**Tags:** substack, growth, collaboration, recommendations, networking

## Core Principle
Build a compounding growth loop through strategic Substack recommendations and reciprocal collaborations — peer trust converts far better than cold social traffic.

## Why It Works
Substack data: recommendations drive ~40% of free subscriptions and ~20% of paid subscriptions across the network. A single well-matched cross-promo delivers 50–100 new subscribers vs. weeks of organic effort.

## Target Selection
- Writers with audience overlap but a different angle: same topic, different approach — audiences are compatible but non-redundant.
- Look for writers at a similar subscriber tier (±2x). Asymmetric swaps get awkward fast.

## Collaboration Ladder (escalating value)
1. Restack, comment, reply to Notes — build visible relationship first
2. Reciprocal recommendation swap (baseline)
3. Guest post swap (each writes one issue for the other)
4. Co-hosted Substack Live session
5. Interview series or joint challenge

## Outreach Protocol
- Read 5+ of their issues before reaching out
- Reference a specific piece in your outreach message
- Propose a concrete format, not a vague "collab"
- Signal-boost them publicly before making the ask

## Maintenance
- Manage the recommendation list dynamically — rotate in new partners as your audience evolves; remove stale ones.
- Track which recommendations actually convert (Substack dashboard shows source attribution).
