# Subscriber Retention Ladder

**Tags:** newsletter, substack, retention, churn, engagement

## Core Principle
Prevent churn by applying the right intervention at each stage of subscriber drift — from onboarding to win-back.

## The Reality
Average newsletter loses 2–3% of subscribers per month. 40% unsubscribe within 30 days of signup. Most churn is preventable with the right sequence.

## The Four Rungs

**Rung 1 — Onboarding (Days 1–7)**
- Welcome email is the highest-open email you'll ever send (50–80% open rate) — use it to establish the promise, cadence, and deliver one piece of immediately useful content.
- Set expectations explicitly: what topics, how often, what makes this different.

**Rung 2 — Warm Maintenance (Months 1–3)**
- Consistent cadence is more important than frequency — pick one and keep it.
- One "personal feel" touch per month: a story, a behind-the-scenes admission, a direct question to readers.

**Rung 3 — Early Drift Detection (Opens Dropping)**
- Don't wait for full churn — send a re-engagement note *before* they go cold.
- Ask a question or offer something specific. Make it feel personal, not automated.

**Rung 4 — Win-Back (No Open in 60+ Days)**
- Direct question: "Still want this? Click to stay." — one-click reconfirm link.
- Accept that some should leave: a clean, engaged list outperforms a large, cold one for delivery and paid conversion.

## Diagnostic Tool
Cohort analysis: segment by signup month, referral source, content type. Identify which cohorts retain vs. churn, then reverse-engineer why.

## The Fundamental Truth
Retention is content-driven. The single strongest predictor of retention is whether the last issue the subscriber read was compelling. Every issue is a retention decision.
