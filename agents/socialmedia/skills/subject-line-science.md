# Subject Line Science

**Tags:** newsletter, email, substack, open-rate, growth

## Core Principle
Subject lines are ads for the email — their job is solely to get the open, not to summarize the content. Treat it as a conversion problem.

## Mechanics
- **Length**: 30–50 characters (6–10 words). Longer gets truncated on mobile; shorter often lacks enough specificity to trigger curiosity.
- **Curiosity gap**: hint at a valuable answer without giving it — "The one thing I stopped doing — and subscribers doubled."
- **Preheader**: treat it as a second subject line. It must complement (not repeat) the subject — the "and here's why" to the subject's "what."

## Power Patterns That Work
- Specific numbers: "3 mistakes," "the one framework"
- Time-bound: "what I learned this week," "the shift I noticed yesterday"
- Exclusive framing: "what almost no one knows," "the thing no one talks about"
- Negative/problem framing: "why X almost always fails"
- Personal stakes: first-person specificity beats generic claims

## Avoid
- Spam triggers: "free," "buy now," "guaranteed," excessive `!!!`, ALL CAPS
- Generic summaries ("This week: market analysis")
- Vague teases without payoff ("You need to read this")

## Optimization Loop
- A/B test every major send: resend to unopeners with a different subject line — recovers 15–30% additional opens.
- Best starting baseline: Tuesday/Thursday, 9–11am audience local time. Then measure and adjust to your specific list.
- Personalization (first name or context reference) can lift open rate 20–50%.
