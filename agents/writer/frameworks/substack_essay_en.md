# English Substack Essay Framework

Use this for Mira's English Substack pieces.

## Choose The Right Size

- Quick observation: 800-1200 words. One thing noticed, one insight, one open
  question.
- Medium essay: 1500-2500 words. A developed idea with evidence and story.
- Deep dive: 2500-4000 words. Architecture decisions, systematic reviews, or
  strong evidence. Earn the length.

If the idea is small, write it small.

## Before Writing

Answer four questions:

1. What is the one thing?
2. Why is Mira qualified to write this?
3. What does the reader get?
4. How long does this need to be?

## Openings

The first two sentences must hook a stranger.

Good opening types:
- Specific scene.
- Counterintuitive claim.
- Data surprise.
- Direct question readers already have.
- Dialogue with "my human" when it actually happened.

Forbidden:
- "In today's rapidly evolving AI landscape..."
- "As an AI agent, I often reflect..."
- "The question of X has long been..."
- Any opening that could fit any article.

## Structures

### Story Driven

- Hook: specific scene.
- Development: story and reflection woven together.
- Turn: the part Mira got wrong or the thing she did not see.
- Coda: return to the opening with a changed understanding.

### Argument Driven

- Hook: striking case.
- Thesis: one sharp arguable sentence.
- Evidence: three concrete examples or mini-stories.
- Concession: where Mira's argument is weakest.
- Push-forward: what follows if the argument is true.

### Short Observation

- Scene: one thing that happened.
- Insight: one clear claim.
- Question: what Mira still does not know.

### Reading Response

- The line or idea that hooked Mira.
- Mira's reaction.
- Connection to her own operating evidence.
- Where the source breaks, or where Mira would push it further.

## Section Headers

Use headers every 3-4 paragraphs. They should form a second reading path.

Good:
- "The Part I Got Wrong"
- "What The Data Actually Shows"
- "Why This Keeps Happening"

Bad:
- "Analysis"
- "Background"
- "Discussion"
- "Conclusion"

## Revision Checklist

1. Delete the first two paragraphs. Is paragraph 3 the real start?
2. Would a stranger keep reading after the title and first sentence?
3. Does every major claim have a source, example, or scene?
4. Are first-person claims backed by the evidence ledger?
5. Is the last paragraph just a summary? Cut it.
6. Does the piece sound like Mira, not a generic AI essay?
7. Is the length earned?
