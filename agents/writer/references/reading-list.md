# Reading List — Writing Craft Benchmarks

Books and essays that improve writing craft. Mira reads excerpts during idle time
and extracts techniques into writer/skills/.

## Craft & Technique
- "On Writing" — Stephen King (writing habits, honest prose, kill your darlings)
- "Bird by Bird" — Anne Lamott (shitty first drafts, short assignments, perfectionism)
- "The Elements of Style" — Strunk & White (economy of language, clarity)
- "Several Short Sentences About Writing" — Verlyn Klinkenborg (sentence-level thinking)
- "Draft No. 4" — John McPhee (structure, the lead, the nut graf)
- "Steering the Craft" — Ursula K. Le Guin (POV, voice, sound of prose)

## Essay & Nonfiction
- "The White Album" — Joan Didion (personal essay as cultural analysis)
- "Consider the Lobster" — David Foster Wallace (footnote voice, moral essays)
- "Slouching Towards Bethlehem" — Joan Didion (new journalism technique)
- "The Journalist and the Murderer" — Janet Malcolm (ethics of narrative)
- "The Art of the Personal Essay" — ed. Phillip Lopate (anthology, range of forms)

## Fiction Technique
- "Aspects of the Novel" — E.M. Forster (flat/round characters, plot vs story)
- "The Art of Fiction" — John Gardner (narrative dream, sentence mastery)
- "Reading Like a Writer" — Francine Prose (close reading for craft)
- "Burning Down the House" — Charles Baxter (subtext, counterpointed scenes)
- "Self-Editing for Fiction Writers" — Browne & King (show vs tell, dialogue mechanics)

## Chinese Writing
- "写作这回事" — Stephen King (Chinese translation + cultural adaptation notes)
- "文学的故乡" — 阎连科 (Chinese literary voice, regional writing)
- "小说课" — 毕飞宇 (close reading of Chinese classics for craft)

## Style & Voice
- "Dreyer's English" — Benjamin Dreyer (modern style guide, practical rules)
- "Sin and Syntax" — Constance Hale (sentence energy, verb power)
- "Spunk & Bite" — Arthur Plotnik (vivid writing, breaking rules purposefully)

## Reading Protocol
When reading a book from this list:
1. Read one chapter/section at a time
2. Extract specific techniques demonstrated (not general advice)
3. Save as writer/skills/ with concrete examples
4. Note which of your own articles could have benefited from the technique
