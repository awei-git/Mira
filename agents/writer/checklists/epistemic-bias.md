# Epistemic Bias Checklist for Tech Narratives

Use this checklist during editorial review of briefings, reading notes, and skill extractions that involve tech-industry claims from social or web sources.

## Activation

Apply when the source material or requested output contains tech-industry claims, especially from social feeds, vendor posts, blogs, newsletters, conference demos, product launches, investor commentary, or web briefings.

## Survivorship-Bias Red Flags

- [ ] A single successful company, product, founder, workflow, or launch is packaged as a generalizable method.
- [ ] The draft treats an unusually visible winner as evidence for how the whole category works.
- [ ] Base rates are missing: how many similar attempts failed, stalled, quietly shut down, or never found distribution?
- [ ] Failure counts are missing: the narrative names the survivor but not the denominator.
- [ ] The story converts hindsight into prescription: because X worked once, others should copy X.

## Platform-Vendor Interest Alignment

- [ ] The claim's truth value perfectly serves the claimant's commercial interest.
- [ ] A platform, vendor, investor, or founder is presented as a neutral observer of its own market.
- [ ] Product capability claims are sourced only to the company selling the product.
- [ ] Market inevitability claims conveniently justify adoption, budget expansion, valuation, or dependency on the claimant's platform.
- [ ] If incentives align too neatly, require independent sourcing, counterexamples, or explicit attribution.

## Demo-vs-Production Gap

- [ ] Claims of abundance, ease, or inevitability rely on demos rather than production evidence.
- [ ] Stability evidence is absent: uptime, error rates, rollback behavior, latency, cost variance, or operational load.
- [ ] Long-chain integration evidence is absent: messy data, existing systems, permissions, audits, compliance, handoffs, and maintenance.
- [ ] Failure-mode evidence is absent: edge cases, user correction loops, monitoring, human review, and recovery from partial failure.
- [ ] A prototype is treated as proof of deployability without showing what breaks after real users, scale, or repeated use.

## Editorial Response

- [ ] Rephrase vendor or platform claims as attributed positions, not settled facts.
- [ ] Add uncertainty when base rates, failure counts, independent sourcing, or production evidence are missing.
- [ ] Distinguish demo evidence from production evidence.
- [ ] Preserve the original claim when it is important, but make its evidentiary status visible.
