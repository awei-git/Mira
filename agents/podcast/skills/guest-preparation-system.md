# Guest Preparation System

**Tags:** podcast, interview, guests, preparation, workflow

## Core Principle
The quality of a podcast interview is largely determined before the recording starts — in how well the host has prepared and how well the guest has been set up to succeed.

## Pre-Interview Research (Host)
**Minimum (any guest)**
- Listen to 3 recent interviews they've given — identify what questions they've answered dozens of times and avoid those
- Read their most recent significant work (book, paper, article, project)
- Find the one thing they believe that most people in their field disagree with — this is often the most valuable thread

**Deep (major guests)**
- Their origin story: what were they doing before they became known for what they're known for?
- Their public contradictions: statements or positions that shifted over time
- What they haven't been asked — not just what they usually talk about

## The Pre-Interview Brief (for the guest)
Send 5–7 days before recording:
- Show concept and audience (1 paragraph)
- Episode angle: what specific story or insight you're trying to draw out from them specifically
- 3–5 question areas (not exact questions) — enough to warm them up, not enough to let them rehearse
- Technical setup requirements (mic recommendation, headphone use, environment)
- Recording logistics: platform, time, duration, recording policy

## The Pre-Call (10–15 min before recording)
- Check audio quality before starting the actual recording
- Re-establish rapport — guests who feel comfortable produce better material
- Give one concrete direction: "I'm going to pause after your answers sometimes — don't fill the silence"
- Set expectations for the edit: "We'll clean up stumbles in post, so don't worry about restarts"

## During the Interview
- Keep a notepad for follow-up threads that arise — don't interrupt the flow to chase them, note them and return
- The best question is often the one the guest's answer just implied but didn't state

## Post-Interview
- Send a thank-you within 24 hours with a rough timeline
- Note what worked and what didn't for each guest type — this calibrates future preparation
