# Audio Editing Discipline

**Tags:** podcast, audio-editing, post-production, workflow, quality

## Core Principle
Audio editing is primarily subtraction — remove what distracts, preserve what carries meaning. The goal is not to make the conversation sound produced; it's to make it sound like the best version of itself.

## What to Remove (in order of priority)
1. **Dead air and excessive pauses** — cut gaps longer than 1.5–2 seconds down to 0.5–0.8s. Natural pauses stay; uncomfortable silences go.
2. **Filler words (selective)** — "um," "uh," "like," "you know." Remove most; leave some. Removing all of them makes speech sound inhuman and clipped.
3. **False starts and stumbles** — cut restarts where the speaker clearly re-begins a thought
4. **Distracting sounds** — mouth noise, chair squeaks, background interruptions, notification chimes
5. **Redundancy** — when a speaker makes the same point twice, keep the better version

## What to Keep
- Laughter, genuine hesitation, emotional moments — these are the humanity of the recording
- Natural breath before important statements — removes these and statements sound rushed
- "Thinking" pauses — "hmm" and brief silences before a considered answer are valuable; they signal reflection

## Edit-to-Transcript Workflow
1. Export transcript (Descript, Otter, or similar)
2. Mark cuts in the transcript — faster than scrubbing audio
3. Make cuts in the DAW — use the transcript markers as a guide
4. Listen back to catch audio artifacts from cuts (clicks, abrupt transitions)

## Fixing Audio Artifact Cuts
After removing material, the edit point often has a click or a tone discontinuity:
- Use a short crossfade (5–20ms) at every edit point
- If removing a filler word mid-sentence, match the room tone on both sides of the cut

## The Listen-Through Test
After editing, listen to the entire episode without touching anything. If you're tempted to stop and fix something, note the timestamp and fix it in a second pass. Never edit while listening through — you'll lose the sense of the whole.
