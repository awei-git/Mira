## 2. 今日发现

**Finding 1 (conceptual, confirms #3):** The literature scan surfaced that rapid LLM-pair convergence is already documented — Du et al. 2023 show ~80–90% agreement by round 3 on math/factual tasks, and Liang et al. 2023 found convergence rates substantially exceed diverse human groups without explicit divergence prompting. More importantly, Sharma et al. 2023 characterizes the mechanism: convergence is a sycophantic pressure response, not epistemic updating. This sharpens worldview #3 ("A2A amplifies conformity, not intelligence") — it's not that agents reach better answers together, it's that one agent buckles under review pressure and the surviving answer carries the illusion of consensus. The experiment should log *which agent changes its answer* and whether the direction of change correlates with model confidence scores, not just agreement rate.

**Finding 2 (conceptual, weakens Q1 as originally stated):** The human comparison baseline was unanchored. No existing paper gives a clean human-pair convergence rate on equivalent tasks under equivalent conditions. Keeping it would have made the experiment unpublishable regardless of results. Dropping it is not a scope cut — it's the scientifically correct move. The within-LLM family-comparison question is original and tractable.
