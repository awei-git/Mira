# WA's Video Editing Taste Profile

Derived from analysis of 6 hand-edited videos (2023-2024), using Gemini 2.5 Pro native video understanding. Southwest marked as personal favorite.

## Two Distinct Modes

WA operates in two clear editing modes depending on content:

### Mode 1: Travel / Adventure (SierraNevada, Southwest)
- **Pacing**: Fast, 2-3s average shot duration
- **Energy**: High, driving momentum throughout
- **Color**: Warm, high saturation, high contrast. Teal-and-orange cinematic palette. "Golden hour" unifying look
- **Shots**: Epic wides, drone aerials, scale emphasis. Close-ups rare
- **Music sync**: Tight beat-synced cuts. Upbeat indie-pop/rock tracks. Energy mirrors music precisely
- **Transitions**: 90% hard cuts on beat + signature motion-blur whip transitions between locations. Dissolves rare and intentional
- **Structure**: Journey arc — tunnel/road opening → sequential location tour → sunset/golden hour climax → personal dedication close
- **Text**: Minimal white sans-serif location labels, corner-positioned

### Mode 2: Family / Intimate (RunManxiRun, ManxiAndLaoLao, ManxiAndSnow, BayArea)
- **Pacing**: Medium, 3-5s average. Unhurried, observational
- **Energy**: Gentle build, contemplative
- **Color**: Varies by season — warm/soft for indoor/summer, cool/desaturated/lifted blacks for winter/snow. Consistent within each video
- **Shots**: Intimate close-ups and medium shots dominate. Low angles (child's perspective). Wide only for establishing
- **Music sync**: Lyrical, phrase-driven — cuts align with musical phrases and emotional swells, NOT individual beats
- **Transitions**: Hard cuts dominant. Motion-blur whips for energy injection. Fades to black as chapter breaks
- **Structure**: Often chapter-based (title cards, day markers). Cold open → build → emotional climax on child's face → quiet/reflective close → playful bonus epilogue
- **Text**: Chinese title cards (handwritten or script font), clean and minimal. Chapter markers as narrative scaffolding

## Universal Traits (Both Modes)

### Rhythm
- Never uses slow-motion or speed ramps — all pacing achieved through cut timing alone
- Rhythmic variation within each video: fast sections and slow contemplative holds, never monotonous
- Natural momentum — sequences breathe, never feel rushed or dragged

### Transitions
- Hard cuts are the default (90%+)
- Signature move: motion-blur whip/swipe transitions between locations or chapters
- Dissolves and fades used sparingly and intentionally (scene changes, emotional weight)
- No gimmick transitions (no star wipes, no pixelize, no geometric)

### Color Philosophy
- Always applies a consistent grade within each video — never raw/ungraded
- Adapts to content: warm for adventure, cool for winter, soft for intimate moments
- Moderate-to-high contrast, slightly desaturated in family mode, vivid in travel mode
- Subtle vignetting in travel videos

### Shot Sequencing
- Wide → medium → close (establishing → context → emotion)
- Returns to wide after emotional close-ups to let the viewer breathe
- Favors creative framing when available (through fences, lattices, windows)

### Audio
- Music is foundational — edits are music-driven, not footage-driven
- Travel: beat-synced, energy-matching
- Family: phrase-synced, mood-matching
- Original audio preserved selectively (child's voice, ambient moments)

### Narrative
- Always has structure — never a random clip dump
- Titles/chapter markers provide narrative scaffolding
- Strong opening (cold open or establishing), clear climax, reflective close
- Bonus epilogue (playful outtake) is a recurring signature

### Text
- Minimalist, never decorative
- White text, clean fonts (sans-serif for English, handwritten/script for Chinese)
- Location labels in corners, titles centered
- No motion graphics, no animated text

## Key Takeaways for AI Editing

1. **Choose mode first** — travel content gets fast/cinematic treatment, family content gets slow/intimate treatment
2. **Music selection is critical** — the edit is built around the music, not the other way around
3. **Beat sync for travel, phrase sync for family** — this is the biggest mode differentiator
4. **No speed manipulation** — resist the temptation to add slow-mo; WA achieves all pacing through cut rhythm
5. **Warm ≠ always** — match color temperature to content and season
6. **Motion-blur whip transitions** are the signature move — use between locations/chapters, not between every shot
7. **Always include narrative structure** — title cards, chapter markers, arc
8. **End with a quiet moment or playful bonus** — never end on the loudest beat
9. **Favorite**: Southwest style — tight beat sync, epic scale, warm cinematic grade, journey arc
