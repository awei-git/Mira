# Visual Continuity

**Tags:** video, editing, continuity, storytelling

## Core Principle
Maintain spatial, temporal, and logical consistency between shots so the viewer's mental model of the scene never breaks.

## Technique
The viewer unconsciously constructs a 3D mental model of the scene. Every cut is a potential break in that model. Continuity editing keeps the model intact:

**Spatial continuity:**
- 180-degree rule: keep the camera on one side of the action line. Crossing it flips screen direction and disorients.
- 30-degree rule: move the camera at least 30 degrees between cuts on the same subject, or it feels like a jump cut.
- Eyeline match: when a character looks at something, the next shot should show what they see from a consistent angle.

**Temporal continuity:**
- Match on action: cut during movement (a hand reaching, a door opening). The motion carries the eye across the cut.
- Overlap or condense time intentionally — never accidentally. Either show the full action or clearly skip it.

**Logical continuity:**
- Props, costumes, lighting must match across cuts in the same scene.
- Establish-then-detail: wide shot first to orient, then close-ups. Jumping to a close-up without establishment is disorienting.

## Application
- Before cutting, ask: "Will the viewer know where they are after this cut?"
- Use match-on-action as the default cut type — it's the most invisible
- Break continuity rules deliberately for effect (jump cuts for anxiety, crossing the line for disorientation), never accidentally
