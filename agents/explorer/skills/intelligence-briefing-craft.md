# Intelligence Briefing Craft

**Tags:** explorer, briefing, writing, analysis, communication

## Core Principle
Translate processed information into actionable briefings using key-judgment-first structure, explicit confidence levels, and implication framing drawn from intelligence community tradecraft.

## Structure
1. **Key Judgment (KJ)** — The single most important conclusion, stated in the first sentence. Must be a *claim*, not a topic ("X is happening" not "about X"). Include confidence level.
2. **Supporting evidence** — Facts that support the KJ. Be specific; no vague hedging.
3. **Counter-evidence / caveats** — What cuts against the KJ or limits its scope.
4. **Implications / So What** — What should the reader do or think differently? If nothing changes, the paragraph shouldn't exist.
5. **Gaps** — What additional information would most change the assessment?

## Confidence Levels (state explicitly, never hide in hedging language)
- **HIGH**: strong sourcing, multiple independent consistent sources
- **MEDIUM**: plausible, limited or single sourcing
- **LOW**: speculation, single anecdote, extrapolation

## Writing Rules
- Write for a reader with 90 seconds. Every briefing = scannable header + 3-5 key bullets + one-paragraph synthesis.
- Call out what is NOT significant: "Despite media coverage of X, this does not change the underlying picture because..."
- Apply the "adversarial reader" test: what is the strongest objection to the KJ? Address it or flag it as a key uncertainty.
- Length is earned by evidence density, not by thoroughness signaling.
