# Weak Signal Detection

**Tags:** explorer, trends, forecasting, horizon-scanning, research

## Core Principle
Identify early-stage indicators of emerging trends before they become obvious, by monitoring anomalous patterns at the periphery of mainstream discourse.

## The Peripheral Scanning Rule
Weak signals almost always arrive from the **periphery** first — adjacent domains, contrarian practitioners, small conferences, preprint servers. Primary feeds confirm; peripheral feeds discover.

## Detection Triggers
- **Cross-domain convergence**: the same phenomenon appears in two or more unrelated domains simultaneously — strongest indicator of a signal becoming a trend.
- **Practitioner-academic gap**: practitioners are doing something that academic literature hasn't caught up to.
- **Outlier results**: findings that contradict the consensus deserve scrutiny, not dismissal.

## Ansoff's Weak Signal Scale
Rate each flagged item 1-5:
1. Threat/opportunity is sensed (vague)
2. Source and nature of threat is known
3. Response options are understood
4. Likely outcomes of responses are known
5. Full strategic response is possible

Escalate items at Level 3+. Discard Level 1-2 unless multiple independent sources agree.

## Second-Order Thinking
Don't flag "AI is growing" — flag "AI growth is changing the economics of X, which will pressure Y." The second-order effect is where the signal has alpha.

## Calibration
Maintain a **weak signal log** — revisit monthly to see which resolved into real trends and which faded. This calibrates future detection sensitivity.
