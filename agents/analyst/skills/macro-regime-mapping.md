# Macro Regime Mapping

**Tags:** analyst, macro, investing, regime, asset-allocation

## Core Principle
Situate any investment thesis within the current macroeconomic regime — understanding which assets and sectors are structurally favored prevents fighting the macro wind.

## The Four Regimes (Dalio's Template)
| Growth | Inflation | Favored assets |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| Rising | Falling | Equities, risk-on |
| Rising | Rising | Commodities, inflation-linked bonds |
| Falling | Rising (stagflation) | Cash, short duration, real assets |
| Falling | Falling | Long bonds, defensives |

## Four Core Determinants to Track
1. **Real GDP growth trajectory** — leading indicators: PMI, yield curve, credit conditions
2. **Inflation trend** — CPI, PPI, breakeven rates, wage growth
3. **Risk premiums** — credit spreads, equity risk premium
4. **Central bank direction** — rate path, QT/QE, forward guidance

## Sector Rotation Logic
- **Expansion**: cyclicals (consumer discretionary, industrials, tech)
- **Contraction**: defensives (utilities, staples, healthcare)
- **Stagflation**: energy, materials, commodities
- **Deflation**: long-duration bonds, gold

## Credit Cycle Overlay
- Yield curve shape (steep vs. inverted)
- Credit spreads (widening = tightening financial conditions)
- Bank lending standards (tightening = leading recession signal)

## Rules
- Re-evaluate regime classification monthly.
- The most dangerous moment is **regime transition** — signals are mixed and models fail.
- Use regime to constrain stock-picking: even great companies underperform in hostile regimes.
