# Earnings Quality Forensics

**Tags:** analyst, accounting, forensics, earnings, due-diligence

## Core Principle
Assess whether reported earnings reflect economic reality or accounting choices, using accrual decomposition and forensic red-flag screening.

## Primary Metrics
- **Quality of Earnings ratio**: Operating Cash Flow ÷ Net Income. Consistently above 1.0 = high quality; below 0.8 = investigate.
- **Sloan accruals ratio**: decompose net income into cash component and accrual component. Large positive accruals predict future earnings deterioration.

## Forensic Checklist
- **Revenue quality**: does AR growth outpace revenue growth? If yes → possible premature recognition.
- **Inventory**: does inventory grow faster than revenue and COGS? Rising inventory in flat demand → demand or margin problem.
- **Beneish M-Score**: 8-variable model (DSRI, GMI, AQI, etc.). Score above -1.78 suggests possible manipulation.
- **Auditor/accounting changes**: frequent changes in estimates, policies, or auditor are red flags.
- **Segment reporting**: changes that obscure margin trends deserve scrutiny.

## 3-5 Year Cross-Check
Compare GAAP earnings to free cash flow over 3-5 years. Sustained divergence that doesn't normalize is a structural red flag, not a timing difference.

## Application
- Run on any company before building a detailed model.
- Use as a secondary check when quarterly earnings beat significantly — "why is cash flow not following?"
